Divergence (vector) • Magnetic Field (vector) = 0

vector field ?

A space containing a cluster of vectors pointing from each point at every direction

● Vectors have value/size and direction, e.g. wind speed and direction

● As an example, the magnetic field around a magnetic object can be represented as a vector field

divergence ?

'Divergence' is applied to a vector field

When we consider divergence, we always consider the vectors flowing in and out of that specified divergence region

magnetic field ?

Maxwell's equation tells us, however, that the divergence of the magnetic field is zero ALWAYS. It is actually telling us that the divergence of any magnetic field of any magnetic object is zero

● This tells us:

- there are no individual sources or sinks

- there is no magnetic monopole

- you cannot have a single magnetic north or magnetic south, and that is why the divergence of a magnetic field is zero
